📅 Training Data Range
📚 Controls the training dataset size — how many years of price history the AI studies.
❓ Why go back to 2015/2018?

More historical data = better trained AI.

  • 2018 gives ~7 years including bull & bear markets
  • 2015 gives ~10 years — even more patterns
  • The AI needs thousands of examples to learn reliable patterns

This is not the same as the LSTM window below.
Think: the textbook you study from vs the paragraph you read before answering a question.

🧠 LSTM Sequence Length
Currently 10 days per prediction input.
❓ Why is this only 1–60 if data goes back to 2015?

These are two completely different things:

ParameterWhat it controls
Training date rangeHow many years of data the AI learns from
LSTM sequence lengthHow many days the AI reads at once to make one prediction

Analogy:
You study 10 years of history books (training data)
When answering, you re-read the last 10 lines (sequence length)

10 days is the sweet spot validated by the base research paper.

The further a model's shape extends toward a corner, the better it scores on that dimension. A model with a large overall area is good across all dimensions — not just one. This lets us compare XGBoost, RandomForest, and LSTM side by side without picking just one metric."

    📖 What does feature importance mean?
    • XGBoost builds decision trees that split on features to make predictions.
    • Feature importance = how many times each indicator was used in splits, weighted by prediction error reduction.
    • High score = the AI trusted that indicator most when deciding Buy/Sell/Hold.
    • This is evidence your indicator choices were validated by the AI itself, not just assumed.
